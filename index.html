Backstory:

Based on the 1910 same-named horror book "Phantom of the Opera", Andrew Lloyd Webber creates this broadway musical which has won over 70 major theatre awards including 3 Olivier Awards, an Evening Standard Award, 7 Tony Awards including Best Musical, 7 Drama Desk Awards and 5 Outer Critic Circle Awards.

Characters:



The Phantom is a mysterious man who is gifted with musical talents but is also cursed because of his deformity on the right side of his face. He wears a wig to hide his grey, almost little hair yet. He is obsessed with the soprano Christine Daaé, and is called "The Angel of Music" by her, but to others he's named "The Angel of Death, Opera Ghost, or The Phantom of the Opera". He's secretive, passionate, and controlling. A musical genius, the Phantom may hide in the walls of the Opera House, but when The Phantom doesn't get what he wants, his rage can be destructive.

The Phantom (Played by Ramin Karimloo in the 25th Anniversary)

Christine Daaé, a Swedish, orphaned young Soprano singer who had lost her passion for singing. In fact, Christine only becomes a singer to honor her dead father. Though she initially impresses no one, one person hears the potential in her voice: an unseen "angel of music" who tutors Christine from behind the walls of the opera house where she now lives. When someone incapacitates the lead soprano, Christine takes her place and stuns everyone with her voice. Thus begins both a successful career and a tangled love life, for Christine has caught the attentions of both the Opera Ghost, her supposed "angel," and Raoul, the Viscount of Chagny.

Christine Daaé (Played by Sierra Boggess in the 25th Anniversary)

Raoul De Chagny, Erik’s rival. A member of one of the noblest families in France, a Vicomte (a French nobleman). Raoul is a surprisingly innocent and passionate young man, willing to prioritize his emotions over his social duties. Christine Daaé's childhood friend, he acts as a foil and contrast to the Phantom. He reveals his nobility of spirit and his devotion to Christine on various occasions. He does not hesitate to put his life at risk for her, thus demonstrating his courage as much as his capacity to put her well-being before his own.

Raoul De Chagny (Played by Hadley Fraiser in the 25th Anniversary)

The new opera managers Monsieur Firmin & Monsieur André, worked in the scrap metal business before becoming managers. They were co-owners of the Opera Populaire after the previous owner's retirement.

Monsieur Firmin & Monsieur André (Played by Barry James and Gareth Snook in the 25th Anniversary)

Carlotta and Piangi, two opera singers, Carlotta is the opera company's prima donna, she despises Christine Daaé and sees the younger woman as a threat to her job. Carlotta and tenor Ubaldo Piangi are lovers; she often turns to him for consolation when things go wrong.

Carlotta and Piangi (Played by Keira Duffy and Wynn Evans in the 25th Anniversary)

Madame Giry and Meg, Meg is Christine's good friend and her personality is much sweeter, caring and innocent showing genuine concern for Christine's claim of an Angel of Music (really the Phantom) coaching her. Madame Giry works as a choreographer of the corps de ballet. She is shown holding a cane, which she uses to beat time with the music, but which she is rarely shown to use otherwise. She's also the box keeper for the Phantom.

Madame Giry and Meg (Played by Liz Robertson and Daisy Maywood in the 25th Anniversary)
